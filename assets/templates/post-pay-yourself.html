Pay Yourself the Right Way

Owner draw, salary, distributions—how to keep it clean and avoid surprises.

By Logical Books • Aug 18, 2025

Sole prop/LLC (taxed as disregarded entity)

You typically take an owner draw—that’s equity, not an expense. Plan a monthly draw and set aside taxes (quarterly estimates are your friend).

S‑Corp

Owners who work in the business usually take a reasonable salary via payroll and may take additional distributions when profits allow. Keep payroll consistent; distributions should follow clean, reconciled months.

Keep it clean

  • Separate accounts: no personal spending on the business card.
  • Decide a cadence: weekly, twice‑monthly, or monthly.
  • Run payroll for salaries; route draws/distributions as equity, not expenses.
