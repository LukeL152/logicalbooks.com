When to Hire a Bookkeeper

Five practical signals it’s time to get help and reclaim your focus.

By Logical Books • Aug 18, 2025

Here are the patterns we see when teams are ready for professional bookkeeping.

  1. Closes slip or never quite “finish.” Month‑end lingers into mid‑month. You’re waiting on reconciliations to answer basic questions.
  2. Founder time drain. You’re spending 4–6+ hours a week in the books. That’s time you could spend on sales or product.
  3. Cash surprises. A bill hits unexpectedly or receivables stack up. Forecasting feels fuzzy.
  4. Tax season stress. Your CPA flags gaps or asks for clarifications that take hours to track down.
  5. Raising or borrowing. Lenders and investors expect clean, accrual‑based statements and consistent processes.

What a bookkeeper actually does

  • Builds a simple monthly rhythm: import, categorize, reconcile, review, and report.
  • Preps useful reports (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow) and answers “what changed?”
  • Sets up light controls so money moves with fewer surprises.

The goal isn’t just tidy books—it’s decision‑ready visibility. If you’re seeing these signals, it’s time. We can stand up a reliable close and keep you focused on growth.
