How to Read Your P&L and Balance Sheet

A founder’s guide to the two core reports—what to scan and what to ask.

By Logical Books • Aug 18, 2025

Profit & Loss (P&L)

The P&L shows revenue and expenses over a period (month, quarter, year). Focus on:

  • Gross margin: Revenue minus cost of goods. Is it steady? Improving?
  • Operating expenses: Where is spend growing faster than revenue?
  • Net income: Profit after all expenses. Trend lines matter more than one month.

Balance Sheet

The balance sheet is a snapshot of what you own and owe on a specific date.

  • Cash: Your oxygen. Compare to your runway estimate.
  • A/R and A/P: Customers who owe you, and bills you owe. Are they aging?
  • Inventory and debt: Watch capital tied up and obligations coming due.

Questions to ask each month

  • What moved the needle this month (up or down) and why?
  • Any one‑time items I shouldn’t project forward?
  • Are there leading indicators (pipeline, churn, unit costs) to watch?
