Build a Simple Cash Runway Forecast

Four steps to see how many months of cash you have—and what to adjust.

By Logical Books • Aug 18, 2025

1) Start with today’s cash

Look at your bank balance(s) and subtract committed payments due this week (payroll, rent, credit cards). That’s your starting cash.

2) Estimate monthly inflows

  • Recurring revenue you expect to collect.
  • Outstanding invoices (apply a realistic collection timing).
  • One‑off cash (refunds, deposits, grants).

3) List your monthly outflows

  • Fixed: payroll, rent, software, insurance, debt payments.
  • Variable: cost of goods, marketing, contractors, travel.
  • Quarterly/annual items: taxes, subscriptions—spread these across months.

4) Do the math

Runway (months) = Current cash ÷ Monthly net burn. If net is positive, great—you’re extending cash. If negative, you have burn.

Actions to extend runway

  • Collect faster: invoice promptly, add ACH, follow up on A/R weekly.
  • Smooth costs: negotiate terms, pause nice‑to‑haves, reduce waste.
  • Sequence hires and big spends to revenue milestones.
