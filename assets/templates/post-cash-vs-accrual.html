Cash vs. Accrual Accounting: What’s the Difference?

Clear, plain‑English guidance to help you choose the right method for your stage.

By Logical Books • Aug 18, 2025

The method you choose determines when revenue and expenses hit your books. It affects taxes, margins, and how “true” your month‑to‑month results look.

Cash accounting (simple and literal)

You record income when money lands in your bank account and expenses when money leaves. It’s easy to maintain and aligns with cash on hand.

  • Great for: early‑stage businesses, solo founders, low complexity.
  • Watch out for: lumpy results if invoices or bills cross month‑end.

Accrual accounting (accurate timing)

You record income when it’s earned (invoice issued or work delivered) and expenses when they’re incurred, regardless of when cash moves. This smooths results and supports clean comparisons.

  • Great for: B2B invoicing, subscriptions, inventory, and growth stages.
  • Watch out for: slightly more process (invoices, A/R, A/P, and cutoffs).

Which should you choose?

If you’re just getting started and need simplicity, cash works fine. If you’re sending invoices, carrying inventory, or sharing numbers with investors or lenders, accrual will tell a clearer story.

Quick decision checklist

  • Do you invoice customers or ship products across month‑end? → Accrual.
  • Running lean, few transactions, want easy tax prep? → Cash.
  • Planning to raise money or borrow? → Accrual will be expected.
